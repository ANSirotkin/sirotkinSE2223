Investigating Trichoderma's Effect on Chlorophytum Comosum (2021 - 2022)

Background

Trichoderma is a genus of fungi that is a member of the family Hypocreaceae. They are green-spored fungi that develop spores in asci (Schuster & Schmoll, 2010). Chlorophytum comosum, also known as “spider plant” is a perennial flowering plant that is considered to be a common houseplant. This study will research the effects of various sizes of Trichoderma populations on the growth rate of C. comosum plants. Trichoderma has been shown to have beneficial effects on plants. Trichoderma strains have been shown to have the ability to increase plant growth rate, plant resistance to various diseases, and plant tolerance to abiotic stresses (Hermosa et al., 2012).

Rationale

Trichoderma is capable of fighting off plant diseases and keeping plants healthy. Understanding the true effect that Trichoderma has on plant growth can lead to proper utilization of Trichoderma in populations of plants such as C. comosum, which may benefit those who rely on plants, such as the people of Kenya who grow C. comosum and use it as a food source (Masinde et al., 2005). In addition, Trichoderma is already being used in large quantities in agriculture to help with disease control and crop yield (Harman et al., 2007). The study’s purpose was to investigate the effects of Trichoderma on C. comosum specimens to assess Trichoderma’s ability to aid a plant’s growth.

Research Practicum

Research Practicum is the final required research course at HTHS. During this course you are required to meet with faculty mentors (and outside mentors if you are working in another facility), conduct your experiments, analyze and interpret your results and draw conclusions relevant to the rationale for your research. You will then write a comprehensive research paper, integrating the knowledge gained in both ITR and this course. At the end of this course all students are required to give a professional quality oral presentation of their work. In addition to research and data analysis skills, you will develop skills in communication, time management, organization, decision making, resource allocation, scientific ethics, and lab safety.
